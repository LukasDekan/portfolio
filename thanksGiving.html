Thanksgiving website

The most colorful time of year is Autumn, a season where leaves peel off from the street, embroidered in different colors, all representing a monochromatic color scheme. This season of color is the leading inspiration behind my Thanksgiving-themed website, one of my favorite CART projects.

To create said site, I first made a mockup inside of Figma to get the general layout and Autumn-themed color scheme planned out. I then made CSS and HTML files inside of a GitHub repository, which I would use to code the website and make it functional. Throughout the process, I ran into the typical problems that any beginner web developer would come across, but with persistence, I managed to make a functioning website that serves its purpose and is aesthetically pleasing to look at. Once I finished, I hosted the project on Netlify for public visibility.

Although the site itself is limited in color, it still shares the warm feeling of Fall, which is achieved through its light, classy font styles and tasty-looking images of Thanksgiving food. The highlight for this website is the navigation menu, which adds to the fancy aesthetics along with being responsive and user-friendly. As with any CART project, I am proud of my work, and I am happy with the result.
